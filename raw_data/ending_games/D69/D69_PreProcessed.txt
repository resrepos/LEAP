A counterpart to this position is found in Diagram 69, which shows one of the few cases in which the possession of an extra pawn does not force a win. It seems at first sight as if White could win by simply assuming the opposition with 1. Ke4 continued: ... Ke7 2. Kd5 Kd7 3. f5 Ke7 4. Kf6 etc. But Black would reply 1. ... f5+ and after 2. gxf5+ Kf6 followed by Kxf5 ensure the draw.
We come now to those endgames in which pieces as well as pawns are left on the board.
As it is my aim to give typical examples, I shall confine myself to positions where there is only one piece besides the King. Most end-games with several pieces can be reduced to that.
